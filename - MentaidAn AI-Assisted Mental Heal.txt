        # üß† **MENTAID: An Explainable, Memory-Retaining, Insight-Generating Mental Health NLP System**
        
        ---
        
        ## üß© Core Goal:
        
        > To create a journaling assistant that detects schizophrenia-linked linguistic patterns over time, using:
        > 
        - NLP transformers (like MentalBERT),
        - SHAP/LIME for interpretability,
        - Psycholinguistic features (via Empath/spaCy),
        - A chunked analysis approach for long-form journaling,
        - **LLM-powered insight summaries**, and
        - A memory engine that **preserves longitudinal trends** and avoids short-term overfitting or fluctuations.
        
        ---
        
        ## üõ†Ô∏è SYSTEM PIPELINE OVERVIEW
        
        ### 1Ô∏è‚É£ **Chunked NLP Inference Engine**
        
        - Journals are broken into overlapping chunks (200‚Äì300 words) to match model training format
        - Each chunk is processed by:
            - **MentalBERT** or classical classifiers for prediction
            - **SHAP/LIME** for local explanation
            - **Empath, spaCy, TextStat** for linguistic & psychological markers
        
        ### Output per chunk:
        
        ```json
        {
          "chunk_id": "chunk_001",
          "pred_label": "schizophrenia",
          "confidence": 0.84,
          "shap_words": ["unreal", "control", "people"],
          "empath": {"confusion": 0.68, "violence": 0.12, "sadness": 0.40}
        }
        
        ```
        
        ---
        
        ### 2Ô∏è‚É£ **Daily Aggregation (Post-chunking)**
        
        Each day‚Äôs entry is aggregated into a structured daily record:
        
        ```json
        {
          "date": "2025-07-04",
          "avg_risk": 0.76,
          "dominant_features": ["confusion", "negativity"],
          "shap_top_words": ["unreal", "people"],
          "highlighted_chunks": [0, 2, 4],
          "nlp_summary": "...",
          "embedding": "<vector>"  // for drift detection
        }
        
        ```
        
        ---
        
        ### 3Ô∏è‚É£ **Temporal Memory & Stability Logic**
        
        - Journal entries are stored **per day** in MongoDB Atlas
        - Aggregations are done **weekly**, **biweekly**, and **monthly**
        - **Rolling summaries** retain historical insight even if a single ‚Äúbetter day‚Äù occurs:
            - Instead of dropping risk immediately after one low-risk entry, Mentaid uses a **trend-weighted average** with thresholds and semantic drift guards
        
        > ‚ú® This protects against false ‚Äúall clear‚Äù signals when improvement is short-lived, and ensures subtle deterioration is still tracked.
        > 
        
        ---
        
        ### 4Ô∏è‚É£ **Semantic Drift Detection**
        
        - Sentence embeddings (`sentence-transformers`) are used to compare journal entries across days
        - High drift may imply:
            - Sudden emotional shifts
            - Language fragmentation
            - Topic derailment
        - These are visualized over time and used to flag "change alerts"
        
        ---
        
        ### 5Ô∏è‚É£ **LLM-Based Insight Generation (Core Differentiator)**
        
        At every major aggregation point (daily, weekly, monthly), Mentaid submits the NLP data to an **LLM (like Mistral 7B via Groq/OpenRouter)** to generate:
        
        ---
        
        ### ‚úçÔ∏è A. **Clinician-Facing Natural Language Summary**:
        
        > ‚ÄúPatient's journaling over the past 2 weeks indicates elevated confusion and disorganized thought markers. Peak risk was observed between July 2 and 4. Linguistic coherence dropped 34% compared to baseline, and top SHAP terms suggest derealization language.‚Äù
        > 
        
        ---
        
        ### üìä B. **Frontend-Usable JSON Report**:

          "alert_days": ["2025-07-02", "2025-07-03", "2025-07-04"],

        ## 1Ô∏è‚É£ **LIWC (Linguistic Inquiry and Word Count)**
        
        üß† **What It Is:**
        
        - Used in thousands of psychology/psychiatry papers
        - Analyzes text for **emotional**, **cognitive**, **biological**, **social**, and **perceptual** word usage
        - E.g., how often does someone use "I", "they", "feel", "sad", "think", etc.
        - Known to reveal underlying mental states
        
        üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏è **In Your App:**
        
        - You use **Empath** + **spaCy** to extract LIWC-style categories like:
            - `confusion`, `negativity`, `violence`, `social`, `sadness`, `self-reference`
        - Trends over time are visualized on:
            - **Radar charts** (this week vs. baseline)
            - **Bar graphs** (daily psych score intensity)
        - Doctors can **see which markers are rising/falling**, not just abstract scores.
        
        ‚úÖ **Clinical Value Provided:**
        
        Shows **psychological function** patterns ‚Üí e.g., increased "confusion" + decreased "social" = early sign of **cognitive withdrawal**.
        
        ---
        
        ## 2Ô∏è‚É£ **PANSS (Positive and Negative Syndrome Scale)**
        
        üß† **What It Is:**
        
        - Core schizophrenia assessment tool
        - Measures **positive symptoms** (hallucinations, delusions)
        - And **negative symptoms** (blunted affect, poverty of speech)
        - Based on **behavior + communication patterns**
        
        üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏è **In Your App:**
        
        - You don‚Äôt measure symptoms directly, BUT:
            - You **infer linguistic signals** associated with PANSS domains
                - e.g., frequent ‚Äúunreal‚Äù ‚Üí derealization
                - semantic drift ‚Üí thought disorganization
                - sparse or repetitive speech ‚Üí alogia-like patterns
        - PANSS-like observations are surfaced as:
            - **Flagged chunks with high confusion scores**
            - **Drift % from past weeks**
            - **SHAP highlight terms (e.g. ‚Äúnothing feels real‚Äù, ‚Äúthey‚Äôre watching‚Äù)**
        
        ‚úÖ **Clinical Value Provided:**
        
        Indirectly identifies **positive/negative symptom clusters** through language ‚Äî helping doctors decide **when to investigate further**.
        
        ---
        
        ## 3Ô∏è‚É£ **PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire - 9)**
        
        üß† **What It Is:**
        
        - A standard depression screener
        - Measures **severity over time**
        - Focuses on patterns across days/weeks
        
        üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏è **In Your App:**
        
        - You track risk **over time**, just like PHQ-9:

        - You don‚Äôt ask questions like PHQ-9 ‚Äî but you infer answers through **journaling behavior**
        
        ‚úÖ **Clinical Value Provided:**
        
        Gives **temporal insight** into emotional state progression ‚Äî allows doctors to track **improvement, deterioration, or relapse risk**.
        
        ---

        üß† **What It Is:**
        
        - Clinical note format used by doctors & therapists
        - **Subjective:** What patient reports
        - **Objective:** What clinician observes
        - **Assessment + Plan:** What the doctor thinks and will do

    - What does a FHIR compliant app look like ?
        
        A **FHIR-compliant app** in mental health is designed to seamlessly **interoperate with healthcare systems** by following the HL7 FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) standards, which enable secure, standardized, and real-time exchange of clinical data between apps and Electronic Health Records (EHRs) like Epic or Cerner[2](https://www.pal.tech/case_study/developed-smart-on-fhir-application-for-behavioral-health-provider-network/)[4](https://www.hashstudioz.com/blog/fhir-hl7-standards-in-healthcare-apps-ensuring-interoperability-compliance/)[5](https://topflightapps.com/ideas/smart-on-fhir-development/).
        
        ## Characteristics of a FHIR-Compliant Mental Health App
        
        - **Seamless EHR Integration:** The app can connect directly to hospital or clinic EHR systems to fetch and update patient data such as demographics, clinical notes, medications, and diagnostic reports without manual data entry[2](https://www.pal.tech/case_study/developed-smart-on-fhir-application-for-behavioral-health-provider-network/)[5](https://topflightapps.com/ideas/smart-on-fhir-development/)[6](https://www.syscreations.ca/blog/mental-health-app-integration-with-ehr/).
        - **SMART on FHIR Framework:** Uses SMART on FHIR protocols for secure user authentication and authorization, allowing clinicians to launch the app within their existing clinical workflow with patient context passed automatically[3](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/smart-app-launch/)[7](https://binariks.com/blog/smart-on-fhir-in-healthcare/).
